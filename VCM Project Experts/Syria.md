Witnessing the devastating impact of war on this rich tapestry, particularly the destruction of historical sites and the displacement of communities, instilled in her a lifelong dedication to finding solutions that are not just scientifically sound but also culturally sensitive and socially equitable, promoting reconciliation and healing alongside environmental responsibility.

After an impressive academic career focused on post-conflict heritage reconstruction and sustainable development in war-torn regions, Dr. Hassan returned to Syria, determined to empower communities to become active stewards of their "watan" (homeland).
